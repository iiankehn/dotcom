ChatGPT may be coming to Apple CarPlay

Published on February 6, 2026 by Iian Kehn


If you've spent any time with the current version of CarPlay, you know that Siri has long been the only voice assistant allowed in the driver's seat. However, a new report from *The Verge* suggests that Apple is finally getting ready to break that monopoly. According to the rumor, an upcoming iOS update could open the door for third-party AI chatbots—like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Claude—to integrate directly with the CarPlay interface. This would mark a significant shift in how Apple manages its automotive ecosystem, potentially allowing drivers to use more advanced conversational AI for hands-free tasks that Siri currently struggles to handle.


The move appears to be a response to the rapid evolution of generative AI and the fact that several automakers are already starting to bake their own AI voice assistants directly into vehicle dashboards. While you can technically use these chatbot apps on your iPhone today, they aren't optimized for the road; this update would reportedly introduce a "voice-enabled" category for CarPlay apps. This would allow users to trigger these assistants through the car's display and microphones, though the report notes that Apple doesn't plan to let you replace Siri entirely. You'd likely still need to open the specific app to use its voice mode, as the system-level "Siri" button and wake word will remain tied to Apple's own assistant.


While Apple is already working on its own major AI overhaul for Siri (including a high-profile partnership with Google), this rumored "open-door" policy suggests a more flexible approach to the dashboard. By allowing these third-party agents a spot on the screen, Apple seems to be prioritizing a safer, more native-feeling experience for users who are already turning to other LLMs for complex queries. There's no official word on whether this will arrive with a mid-cycle iOS 26 update or be saved for the debut of iOS 27, but it's a clear signal that the passenger-side experience is about to get a lot more talkative.


Meta blames Apple for failure

Published on February 5, 2026 by Iian Kehn


It's no secret that there's no love lost between Meta and Apple, but the latest report from AppleInsider reveals that Mark Zuckerberg's frustrations with Cupertino are deeper and more personal than just a disagreement over privacy settings. Newly surfaced internal emails, which came to light through a lawsuit filed by the New Mexico Attorney General, show Zuckerberg complaining to his leadership team about what he perceives as a massive double standard. He argues that while Meta is constantly under the microscope for its impact on teen safety and mental health, Apple essentially gets a "free pass" for iMessage, which he claims facilitates similar behaviors without the same level of public or regulatory scrutiny.


The internal documents highlight a specific grievance: Zuckerberg feels that because iMessage is a private messaging service rather than a public-facing social feed, it escapes the "intense scrutiny" Meta faces regarding content moderation and child safety. In the emails, he expresses frustration that Meta is forced to spend billions on safety and human reviewers while Apple, in his view, doesn't have to account for how its tools are used by minors. He goes as far as to suggest that Apple isn't even looking for these problems, which allows them to maintain a cleaner reputation in the eyes of the public and lawmakers.


However, the article notes that Zuckerberg's comparison might be ignoring some fundamental differences in how these platforms operate. While Meta's business model is built on algorithmic engagement and public sharing, Apple's iMessage is an end-to-end encrypted communication tool. The report also points out that Apple actually has implemented several safety features, such as Communication Safety tools that blur sensitive content for minors, which contradicts Zuckerberg's internal claims that Apple does "nothing." Ultimately, the piece paints a picture of a Meta CEO who feels unfairly targeted and is increasingly vocal—at least behind closed doors—about shifting the narrative toward his rivals.


Apple launches Creator Studio

Published on January 28, 2026 by Iian Kehn

Apple launched Creator Studio today for iPad and Mac today for users who are looking to take advantage of all Apple's pro level apps all in one subscription. This new subscription is available for individuals (with up to 5 family members using Family Share) and Students.


The Individual Subscription is $12.99/month or $129/year or $2.99/month or $29/year for Students. The suite includes the following pro apps.

  • Final Cut Pro
  • Motion
  • Compressor
  • Logic Pro
  • MainStage
  • Pixelmator Pro

It also includes new templates and features for the following productivity apps

  • Numbers
  • Pages
  • Keynote
  • Freeform

It is available now in the App Store, and Apple is offering a 30-day trial for those who are wishing to take advantage of it.
