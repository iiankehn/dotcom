We need to talk

Posted on Thursday, March 28, 2024 by Iian Kehn

It's been a while since we last spoke, and I don't know where to begin. The last time I wrote a blog post was when I decided to stop writing content and producing video content. So now it's time for me to give you all an update.

Setting expectations

It's been a while since I did anything with my content. I wrote my last post back in January and posted my last video before the holidays. While that might sound crazy to some of you, I needed the break. Work up until this point has been non-stop and I haven't made the time to work on anything.

My mental health took a nosedive during the holidays because of the long hours at work, and I felt that after it was over I needed to step away from writing and producing content on social media. It worked out for the best because the landscape is starting to change and now, we're all figuring out what the future of content looks like.

If you haven't been paying attention to the news, the government is trying to shut down TikTok and not for the reasons you might imagine. The government has been “hard” at work ensuring that the “people” don't have to have their data “stolen by China”, when their real motive is censorship. While there is an argument to be made about ByteDance's motives, millions of people who use the service every single day to make a living are about to lose their livelihood.

Yes, YouTube, Snapchat, Instagram, Twitter, and Facebook do exist. But those platforms were never intended to serve that kind of content. Let's also be honest with ourselves, Meta is garbage, YouTube has a content problem, and Snapchat is irrelevant. If for any reason, TikTok is shut down in the United States, odds are no one is going to step up to the plate and replace it with something better.

What is my plan?

Enough semantics. It's time I break down the plan for my content going forward.

  • YouTube is a dedicated video platform, both short content and long-form content will be hosted there.
  • Videos will resume in mid-April.
  • Existing content will be downloaded from TikTok and uploaded to YouTube (ideally without the Watermark).
  • Written content will exist in the form of guides and reviews. Regular tech news will not be returning.

Why YouTube?

Although I stated that YouTube has a content problem, and a host of other issues, it is the only dominant platform out there that hosts video content on the internet. YouTube is where I started, and likely will be where I stay. YouTube is a stable platform that allows creators to post regular content without government interference because it is run by a company in the United States (although, there has been some scrutiny over antitrust issues).

YouTube also allows me to be more flexible with my content and plan content, something TikTok often lacks. It's going to take some time to rebuild my community of followers, but with time, I think I can make it happen.

What kind of content will I see?

I am going back to vlogging, and talking head videos. If there is one thing I have learned is that scripted videos, at least in my case, don't work for my channel. It's not only time-consuming to make a video that is scripted, but the turnaround for those videos generally doesn't meet the requirements for the kind of content I make. I want to continue posting content about technology, but my focus is going to be on Prosumer Information Tech (in other words, home lab content).

Here's a general list of topics you should expect to see:

  • Management tools that make automating your workflows easier.
  • Tools to help administer your home lab.
  • Configurations, installations, and setup videos.
  • Personal Life Updates, Vlogs, and general tech videos.

What you won't see:

  • Technology event and news coverage
  • General consumer product reviews (unless they tie into the theme of the channel)

Wrapping up

It's been a tough few months, but I am optimistic about the future. I know I lost a majority of my audience after I stopped posting, but burn out is real, and I am not the only one that is feeling this way. Content creation is a hustle, and if you don't take time for yourself, you could end up like many others. I appreciate all the support you guys have given me, and I hope to see you all very soon.
